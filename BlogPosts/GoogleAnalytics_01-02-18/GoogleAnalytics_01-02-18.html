Using Google analytics to track visitors 01-02-18

Joanna and I are headed to Glasgow today, Joanna's VIVA is tomorrow. Whilst Joanna is giving her thesis a read, I'm working away on this post.

Vain as I am, I'm interested in whether anyone is visiting this website. After some searching I found that Google Analytics will do this for you.

The first thing you'll need to do is sign up to Google Analytics, which you uses a Google (gmail, drive, etc.) account. Click on the "Sign Up" button in the topright.

Next you'll need to fill out some details about your account and the website you want to track. Once completed you'll be provided with a tracking code, which you'll need to add to each of the html pages of your website. The tracking code is pasted into the html with a few additional bits of information:

<script async src="https://www.googletagmanager.com/gtag/js?id=[TRACKING-CODE-HERE]"></script>

<script>
    window.dataLayer = window.dataLayer || [];
    function gtag(){dataLayer.push(arguments);}
    gtag('js', new Date());
    gtag('config', '[TRACKING-CODE-HERE]');
</script>

Google Analytics provides the above code on their page. Just select it and copy it into your html files into the header region, at the top:

<html>

    <head>
        ... <- HERE
    </head>

    ...

That's you up and running. Now, on the Google Analytics home page you'll be able to monitor how visitors your webpage has (not many for me!), where they are from, and what they are looking at.
